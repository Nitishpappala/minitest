GPS Spoofing Detection System

Understanding vulnerabilities in satellite navigation and countering malicious signal tampering.

What is GPS Spoofing?

GPS Spoofing is a sophisticated technique where false satellite signals are generated to mislead GPS receivers. Spoofing can cause systems — including drones, autonomous vehicles, and ships — to compute false positions and velocities. This manipulation can allow attackers to redirect navigation systems, disrupt autonomous behavior, or hijack vehicles entirely.

Unlike jamming, which blocks signals entirely, spoofing subtly feeds fake information to the victim, maintaining the illusion of normalcy. Attackers can either overlay legitimate signals or completely replace them to achieve their objectives.

How Spoofing Impacts UAVs & Autonomous Systems

Modern UAVs (drones) rely on a fusion of sensors — GPS, IMUs (Inertial Measurement Units), vision, and sometimes LiDAR. However, GPS remains the primary global positioning system due to its worldwide coverage. If an attacker successfully broadcasts spoofed signals, the UAV may drift toward false locations, enter no-fly zones, or even lose control entirely.

In the real world, incidents of GPS spoofing have caused both civilian and military drones to land unexpectedly or crash. Research presented at the USENIX Security Symposium has shown how a low-cost spoofing setup can be used to mislead drones without touching their software.

Real-World GPS Spoofing Incidents

GPS spoofing has been used in various military and civilian incidents. Ships have reported sudden changes in location while anchored, and multiple UAVs have been reported lost due to GPS manipulation. Military-grade spoofing tools are capable of seamlessly transitioning from authentic signals to spoofed ones, making detection extremely challenging in real-time.

Such attacks highlight the need for robust GPS anomaly detection tools — both at the software and hardware level. Organizations such as the US Department of Homeland Security have recognized GPS spoofing as a critical infrastructure threat.

Detecting and Defending Against GPS Spoofing

Defending against GPS spoofing is challenging, especially when the attacker can generate high-quality counterfeit signals. However, detection is possible by observing anomalies in signal parameters such as:

  • Sudden changes in signal strength (CN0)
  • Carrier phase irregularities
  • Position jumps without corresponding velocity changes
  • Time drift inconsistencies

Combining sensor fusion, AI-driven anomaly detection, and hardware signal authentication offers the best chance of preventing spoofing.
